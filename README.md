# Myntra-Sales-Analysis
# Objective:
This project analyzes a Myntra sales dataset to uncover key business and product insights across brands, pricing, ratings, and discounts. It provides exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualizations, and metrics useful for merchandising, pricing strategy, inventory decisions, and understanding customer preferences.

# Key Features of Dataset:
- brand_name  - Clothing brand (e.g., WROGN, Levi's, Roadster)
- pants_description -	Product title / jeans description
- price -	Current selling price (after discount)
- MRP -	Original price before discount
- discount_percent - Discount applied (calculated as (MRP - price) / MRP)
- ratings - Average user rating
- number_of_ratings - Count of reviewers / ratings received

# KPI's:
- Top brands by Product count
- Premium Brand
- Budget Brand
- Most Discounted products
- Most Rated Products
- Most Expensive Products etc.

# Process: 
- Data Gathering & Cleaning - Gather Myntra Sales dataset and clean it by handling missing values, standardizing names, and formatting columns.
- Define Key Metrics (KPIs) - Focus on insights like Highest Priced Product, Most Discounted Product, Number of Unique Brands and Most Rated Product.
- Dashboard Design – Designed multiple dashboard pages including overview, Brand Performance, and Product Insights, also used visual elements like bar charts, pie charts, KPI cards.
- Interactivity & Filters - Added slicers for brand name and used bookmarks and navigation buttons to switch between dashboard pages smoothly.

# Recommendations:
- Majority of items fall in range between ₹1,000 – ₹1,999.
- Most products fall in the 40–60% discount bucket.
- WROGN, Flying Machine, United Colors of Benetton, Roadster, and Mufti have deep catalogs, implying strong sales focus and wide audience targeting.
- Jacob Cohen is one of the most expensive and also shows top selling revenue per item.
- Slim Fit, Skinny Fit, and Mid-Rise dominate top-selling and most-rated lists.
